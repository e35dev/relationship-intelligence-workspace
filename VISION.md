# Relationship Intelligence Workspace

Help boutique investment firms understand, manage, and act on their most important relationships across investors, founders, intermediaries, advisors, and portfolio or deal contacts.

## Who it serves
Founders at boutique investment firms who need a clear view of relationship coverage and next best actions., Investor relations leads who manage outreach, follow-ups, meeting context, and relationship health., Analysts who research contacts, prepare briefing context, and maintain relationship data quality.

## Optimize toward
A focused relationship intelligence workspace that gives boutique investment teams a shared, trustworthy view of their network and turns scattered relationship context into clear next actions.

## Accept work that
- Start from a simple relationship dashboard that shows priority contacts, firms, recent interactions, stale relationships, upcoming follow-ups, and high-value opportunities requiring attention.
- Search or open a person, firm, or opportunity profile to see a concise relationship history, known connections, notes, last touch, next step, relationship strength, and relevant context for the next conversation.
- Prepare for a meeting by generating a briefing view that summarizes who the firm knows, recent activity, open asks, likely interests, and recommended talking points.
- Capture a new interaction quickly after a call, meeting, email, or event, then turn it into structured next steps, reminders, updated relationship strength, and surfaced opportunities.
- Review coverage across a target investor, founder, or intermediary list to identify who owns each relationship, where engagement is weak, and which high-priority contacts need action.
- Use natural-language steering to ask questions such as who has not been contacted recently, which LP relationships are warming, or what follow-ups are due this week, and receive actionable answers tied to relationship records.

## Close work that
- Do not expose internal development, repository, contribution, review, or automation mechanics in customer-facing UI.
- Do not make the normal user manage technical workflow concepts or implementation details.
- Do not build a generic CRM feel that treats every contact as interchangeable; the product must emphasize investment-firm relationship intelligence.
- Do not overbrand the experience or make the product name central before the name is settled.
- Do not use broad AI claims without tying them to concrete relationship workflows and decisions.

## Keep it feeling
- Professional, restrained, and information-dense enough for finance users without feeling like enterprise clutter.
- Customer-facing language should be direct and domain-specific: relationships, firms, contacts, meetings, follow-ups, coverage, and opportunities.
- Prioritize fast scanning, clean tables, compact profiles, and clear status indicators over marketing-style pages.
- The product should feel like a serious operating tool for high-context relationship work.
